The Story of Reflecting

Skye's take on the EDA experience A foot accustomed to running over roots makes the speediest runner. Impressions

15th July 2017

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience? My take is that it's an excelarated learning experience that focuses on enabling students to develop the basic skills needed to get their first job as a developer. With alot of technical content, there is also a wide focus on engineering empathy and soft skills. From attending the 'day in the life' at EDA i liked their views on education in that they view all sides of a person are dynamic and work together and are influenced when learning new skills. I'm a qualified Primary School teacher and when doing my teacher training this view of education including the whole self reminded me of how the Maori culture views education and places like Te Waananga's are about all of someones Hauora (well-being) including spiritual, emotional, physical, mental in comparison to a western/modern view of education which can tend to compartmentalize individuals and not cater to all of their needs. What are your impressions? My impression so far are positive, I like the space on Cuba Street, I was not sure how well I would cope with remote learning but so far have found there is alot of support if and when you need it while working remotely. How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture? I see myself engaging positively in this type of culture. I enjoy learning new skills and have a safe and calm environment to learn in is so important and I believe EDA has endevoured to set this up effectively. Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how? Its only been less than a week so they haven't changed too much so far. Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous? It makes me nervous that I will be lacking in technical knowledge but excited to learn more.
