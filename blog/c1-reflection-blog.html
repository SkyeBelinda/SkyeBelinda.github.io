Skye's take on the EDA experience A foot accustomed to running over roots makes the speediest runner. Impressions

15th July 2017

My take is that it's an excelarated learning experience that focuses on enabling students to develop the basic skills needed to get their first job as a developer. With a focus on developing technical skills and abilities, there is also an equal emphasis on engineering empathy and soft skills needed for web developers. From attending the 'day in the life' at EDA I enjoyed hearing about their values views on education; and how when learning new skills all sides of a person (physical, mental, emotional, spiritual) are dynamic and work together and are influenced when learning new skills. I'm a qualified Primary School teacher and when doing my teacher training this view of education and learning being influened by the whole self reminded me Maori culture views on education and embracing all assets all of someones Hauora (well-being) including spiritual, emotional, physical, mental in comparison to a western/modern view of education which can tend to compartmentalize individuals and not cater to all of their needs.

I see myself engaging positively in this type of culture. I enjoy learning new skills and have a safe and calm environment to learn in is so important and I believe EDA has endevoured to set this up effectively.

Its only been less than a week so they haven't changed too much so far. Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous? It makes me nervous that I will be lacking in technical knowledge but excited to learn more.
